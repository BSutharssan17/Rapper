Physics and Maths

Introduction

Physics and mathematics are fundamental sciences that help us understand the universe. Physics deals with the properties and interactions of matter and energy, while mathematics provides the language and tools to describe these phenomena.

Key Concepts in Physics(GCSE and A-Levels)

  • Newton's Laws of Motion: Describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting on it.
  • Thermodynamics: The study of heat, energy, and work.
  • Electromagnetism: The interaction of electric charges and magnetic fields.
  • Quantum Mechanics: The branch of physics that deals with the behavior of particles at atomic and subatomic levels.

Key concepts in A-Level Maths and GCSE Higher and Foundation Maths

  • Calculus: A branch of mathematics that studies continuous change.
  • Linear Algebra: The study of vectors, vector spaces, and linear transformations.
  • Differential Equations: Equations involving derivatives that describe various physical phenomena.
  • Statistics: The science of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, presenting, and organizing data.
